# Product Requirements Document — ToolVault

**Version:** v0.3.0
**Status:** Active
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-09

---

## 1. Executive Summary

ToolVault is an engineering infrastructure platform providing professional
equipment, collaborative workspaces, expert mentorship, and end-to-end
prototyping support. The website serves as the primary public-facing digital
presence communicating ToolVault's vision to judges, investors, mentors,
educational institutions, startups, and future customers.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Builders, students, and early-stage startups cannot access professional
engineering infrastructure due to:

- Prohibitive equipment costs (250K+ for a basic lab setup)
- Access locked behind institutions (universities, corporations)
- Delayed time to prototype (average 6 months without infrastructure)

---

## 3. Solution Overview

ToolVault provides a complete innovation ecosystem under one roof:

- Professional-grade engineering equipment
- Collaborative workspaces and purpose-built labs
- Expert mentorship and structured guidance
- End-to-end prototyping pipelines
- Builder community and workshops

---

## 4. Target Audience

### 4.1 Primary Users

- Engineering students and recent graduates
- Independent builders and makers
- Early-stage hardware startups
- Researchers and academics

### 4.2 Secondary Users

- Investors and venture capital firms
- University partnerships
- Corporate innovation teams
- Government and institutional partners

## 5. Goals and Objectives

### Website Goals

- Communicate ToolVault vision within 5 seconds
- Convert visitors into community members

- Serve as the foundation for Sprint 4 feature expansion

### Business Goals

- Validate demand before physical launch
- Build founding member waitlist
- Attract strategic partnerships
- Secure seed funding

## 6. Homepage Information Architecture

1. Navigation — Sticky, transparent-to-solid scroll behavior
2. Hero — Full viewport, primary headline, dual CTA
3. Problem — Three problem cards with statistics
4. Solution — Process flow and capability cards
5. Inside ToolVault — Six lab feature cards
6. Capabilities — Seven capability cards with coming soon state
7. Why ToolVault — Six pillar cards
8. Vision — Statement + alternating timeline
9. Roadmap — Five phase cards with status indicators
10. CTA — Inspiring close with founding principle quote
11. Footer — Navigation, social, founder note
